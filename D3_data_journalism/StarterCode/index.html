Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

The latest data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Facor Surveillance System show relationships between the median age and income of a state's population and behavioral risk factors such as smoking and obesity.

Furthermore, states with lower median income levels and higher poverty rates tend to have higher rates of smoking and obesity. The same low-income states tend to have a higher proportion of their population that lacks adequate healthcare.
